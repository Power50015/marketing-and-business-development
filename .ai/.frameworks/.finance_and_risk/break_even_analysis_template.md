## Before You Start — Critical Instructions

> **The single most important rule of Break-Even Analysis:**
> _"You must strictly separate Fixed Costs from Variable Costs. A Fixed Cost (like rent) happens even if you sell zero units. A Variable Cost (like packaging) only happens when you sell a unit. If you mix these up, your break-even point will be completely wrong, and you may accidentally price your product at a loss."_
> _(Source: U.S. Small Business Administration [SBA] — Financial Planning; Jumpstart Partners, 2025)_

**How to use this template:**

**Step 1** — Identify and sum all your **Fixed Costs** for a specific period (e.g., monthly or annually).
**Step 2** — Identify your **Variable Cost per Unit** (how much it costs to produce exactly _one_ item).
**Step 3** — Determine your **Selling Price per Unit**.
**Step 4** — Calculate the **Contribution Margin** (Price - Variable Cost).
**Step 5** — Calculate the **Break-Even Point** (Fixed Costs / Contribution Margin).

> ⚠️ **Research warning:** Do not assume a break-even point guarantees a healthy business. You must calculate your **Margin of Safety** (how far sales can drop before you start losing money). In 2025's volatile market, a margin of safety below 15-20% is considered high-risk.
> _(Source: Xero — Margin of Safety Analysis, 2025)_

## When to Use This Framework

| Situation                    | How This Template Helps                                                                                                  |
| ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Starting a New Business      | Answers the question: "Can we realistically sell enough of this product to survive?"                                     |
| Launching a New Product Line | Determines if the new product can cover its own R&D and marketing costs                                                  |
| Evaluating a Price Discount  | Proves mathematically why offering a 20% discount might require you to sell 50% more volume just to make the same profit |

_(Source: SBA — Financial Planning; ProjectManager — Break-Even Analysis, 2025)_

---

## Framework Limitations — Know What It Cannot Do

| Limitation                 | What to Use Instead                                                                                                                                                                              |
| -------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Ignores Market Demand      | Break-even tells you how many units you _need_ to sell. It does not tell you if the market actually _wants_ to buy that many. Use **Market Research / Product-Market Fit Analysis**.             |
| Assumes Fixed Prices/Costs | In reality, if you buy materials in bulk, variable costs drop. If you sell more, you might discount the price. Use dynamic **Financial Modeling (Excel/DCF)** for complex, multi-tiered pricing. |
