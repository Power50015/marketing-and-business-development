## Before You Start — Critical Instructions

> **The single most important rule of the Lean Canvas:**
> *"The Lean Canvas is not a business plan; it is a hypothesis log. Your first canvas is almost certainly wrong. Its purpose is to force you to write down your riskiest assumptions so you can go out and test them. Love the problem, not your solution."*
> *(Source: Ash Maurya — Lean Canvas Creator; Gonogo Team — Early Stage Validation, 2025)*

**How to use this template:**

**Step 1** — Define the **Problem** and the **Customer Segments** first. If these two don't align, nothing else matters.
**Step 2** — Define your **Unique Value Proposition (UVP)**. Why you?
**Step 3** — Define the **Solution** (Keep it simple — MVP level).
**Step 4** — Identify your **Channels** (How will you reach them?).
**Step 5** — Define the financials: **Revenue Streams** and **Cost Structure**.
**Step 6** — Identify your **Key Metrics** (How will you measure success?).
**Step 7** — Define your **Unfair Advantage** (What protects you from competitors?).

> ⚠️ **Research warning (Lean Canvas vs. Business Model Canvas):** Use the Lean Canvas if you are an early-stage startup trying to find Product-Market Fit. Use the Business Model Canvas if you are an established business trying to scale or map complex operations. The Lean Canvas focuses heavily on the *Problem* and *Metrics*, stripping away operational logistics like "Key Partners" which distract early-stage founders.
> *(Source: Linden Innovation — Framework Differences, 2025)*

> 🔁 **Review cycle:** Review and update your Lean Canvas **every week or after every major batch of customer interviews**. It must evolve rapidly as you invalidate your early assumptions.

## SYNTHESIS: THE RISK ASSESSMENT

*The Lean Canvas is designed to highlight risk. Which of the 9 blocks represents the biggest threat to your startup right now?*

| Risk Type | Where on the canvas? | What is our biggest assumption? | How will we test it this week? |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Product Risk** | Problem / Solution | *(e.g., "Do they actually care about this problem?")* | |
| **Market Risk** | Customer / Channels / UVP | *(e.g., "Can we reach them cheaply?")* | |
| **Financial Risk** | Revenue / Costs | *(e.g., "Will they pay $50/mo for this?")* | |

## When to Use This Framework

| Situation | How This Template Helps |
|---|---|
| Idea Validation | Forces you to deconstruct an idea into its core assumptions before spending money |
| Pitching to early-stage investors | Shows investors you understand your risks, metrics, and unit economics |
| Finding Product-Market Fit | Keeps the team laser-focused on the Problem rather than obsessing over the Solution (features) |
| Weekly founder alignment | Use it as a dashboard to review what assumptions were invalidated this week |

*(Source: Startupply — Lean Canvas Use Cases, 2025; Gonogo Team — Early Stage Startups, 2025)*

---

## Framework Limitations — Know What It Cannot Do

| Limitation | What to Use Instead |
|---|---|
| Not suitable for established, scaling businesses with complex operations | Use the **Business Model Canvas (BMC)** |
| Doesn't map the specific external market forces or competitive landscape | Combine with **Porter's Five Forces** or **PESTLE Analysis** |
| Doesn't detail the specific customer psychology or emotional jobs | Combine with the **Value Proposition Canvas** and **JTBD Framework** |
