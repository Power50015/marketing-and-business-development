### Rebuilt from Primary Research | Sources: Dr. John Kotter (Harvard Business School) · Prosci

## Before You Start — Critical Instructions

> **The single most important rule of Kotter's 8 Steps:**
> *"Do not skip steps. The steps must be followed sequentially. If you try to jump to Step 3 (Vision) without first creating Step 1 (Urgency) and Step 2 (Guiding Coalition), the organization will reject the change. Transformation is a process, not an event."*
> *(Source: John Kotter — Leading Change; Prosci — Change Management, 2025)*

**How to use this template:**

**Step 1** — Define the overall change initiative you are trying to implement.
**Step 2** — Work through the 8 steps sequentially, answering the diagnostic questions.
**Step 3** — Assign owners and deadlines to the action items in each step.
**Step 4** — Do not move to the next phase until the previous phase's objectives are clearly met.

> ⚠️ **Research warning:** In 2025, "Change Fatigue" is the #1 killer of transformations. You must use **Step 6 (Short-Term Wins)** to inject energy into the team. If people don't see tangible, positive results within the first few months, they will revert to their old habits.
> *(Source: VisualSP — Organizational Transformation, 2025)*

## PHASE 3: IMPLEMENTING AND SUSTAINING FOR CHANGE

### 7. Sustain Acceleration (Consolidate Gains)
*Use the credibility from early wins to tackle bigger systemic issues. Don't declare victory too soon.*
- **What are the larger, more difficult phases of this change that we need to tackle next?**
  >
- **Diagnostic Question:** Have we stopped celebrating the initial launch and focused on continuous improvement? *(Yes/No)*

### 8. Anchor New Approaches in the Culture
*Ensure the change becomes permanent ("the way we do things around here").*
- **How will we update our hiring/onboarding process to reflect this change?**
  >
- **How will we update performance reviews to reward the new behaviors?**

## When to Use This Framework

| Situation | How This Template Helps |
|---|---|
| Mergers & Acquisitions | Provides a structured way to combine two different company cultures without causing a mass exodus of staff |
| Digital Transformations | Shifts the focus from the software (the tool) to the people (the users who have to adopt the tool) |
| Failing Initiatives | Use it as a diagnostic tool: "Why did our agile rollout fail? Ah, we skipped Step 1 (Urgency) and Step 5 (Removing Barriers)." |

*(Source: BiteSizeLearning — Change Models, 2025; DanielLock — Kotter Application, 2025)*

---

## Framework Limitations — Know What It Cannot Do

| Limitation | What to Use Instead |
|---|---|
| Too rigid for highly turbulent, startup environments | Combine Kotter's principles with **Agile Methodology** or **Design Thinking** for rapid iteration |
| Does not map the specific daily tasks of a project | Use a **Gantt Chart** or **Kanban Board** to manage the actual execution of the project deliverables |
| Top-Down approach | Can feel hierarchical. For grassroots or bottom-up change, consider the **ADKAR Model** (Awareness, Desire, Knowledge, Ability, Reinforcement) which focuses purely on individual change. |
