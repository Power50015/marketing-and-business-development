## Before You Start — Critical Instructions

> **The single most important rule of the STP Framework:**
> *"If you try to be everything to everyone, you will be nothing to anyone. The STP framework forces you to make hard choices about who you will NOT serve, so you can be extraordinary for the people you DO serve. Segmentation is the analysis; Targeting is the decision; Positioning is the execution."*
> *(Source: Soren Kaplan — Strategic Frameworks, 2025; Smart Insights — STP Marketing Model, 2025)*

**How to use this template:**

**Step 1** — **Segmentation:** Divide your total market into distinct groups with shared needs, behaviors, or characteristics.
**Step 2** — **Targeting:** Evaluate the attractiveness of each segment and explicitly choose which one(s) to focus your resources on.
**Step 3** — **Positioning:** Define how you want your brand to be perceived by that specific target audience compared to competitors.
**Step 4** — Create your **Positioning Statement** to guide all marketing and product decisions.

> ⚠️ **Research warning:** The most common failure in STP is "demographic laziness"—segmenting only by age, gender, or income. In 2025, buying behavior is driven far more by *psychographics* (values/lifestyle) and *behavioral data* (how they use products) than pure demographics.
> *(Source: Blueprint Demand — STP in Modern Markets, 2025)*

> 🔁 **Review cycle:** Review your STP strategy **annually** or whenever entering a new market. Competitors move, and consumer preferences shift. Your positioning must evolve with them.

## STAGE 2: TARGETING (The "Where to Focus")

*Targeting is about evaluation and selection. You cannot serve all segments equally. Which segment offers the best return on investment?*

### 1. Evaluate Segment Attractiveness
*Rate each segment from 1 (Low) to 5 (High) based on the following criteria.*

| Criteria | Definition | Segment A | Segment B | Segment C |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Size & Growth** | Is the segment large enough to be profitable? Is it growing? | | | |
| **Profitability** | Are they willing to pay? What is the lifetime value (LTV)? | | | |
| **Reachability** | Can we easily reach them with our marketing channels? | | | |
| **Competitive Intensity** | Are competitors already dominating this segment? (5 = Low competition) | | | |
| **Strategic Fit** | Does serving them align with our current capabilities and brand? | | | |
| **Total Score** | | **/25** | **/25** | **/25** |

### 2. Select Your Targeting Strategy

☐ **Undifferentiated (Mass Market):** Targeting the whole market with one offer. *(Warning: Rarely works in today's highly fragmented markets).*
☐ **Differentiated:** Targeting multiple segments with unique offerings and campaigns for each. *(Requires high resources).*
☐ **Concentrated (Niche):** Focusing all resources on one specific, highly profitable segment. *(Best for startups and new entrants).*
☐ **Micromarketing / ABM:** Targeting at the individual or individual-account level. *(Common in high-value B2B).*

## When to Use This Framework

| Situation | How This Template Helps |
|---|---|
| Launching a new product | Ensures you know exactly who you are building for and how to message them |
| Entering a highly competitive market | Helps find "white space" (uncontested niches) through the perceptual map |
| Declining marketing ROI | Forces a shift from expensive mass-marketing to efficient targeted marketing |
| Brand repositioning | Provides a structured way to redefine your value in the minds of consumers |
| Scaling a startup | Helps transition from "selling to anyone who will buy" to a focused, scalable go-to-market strategy |

*(Source: Smart Insights — STP Applications, 2025)*

## Framework Limitations — Know What It Cannot Do

| Limitation | What to Use Instead |
|---|---|
| Does not map the specific steps a customer takes to buy | Combine with **Customer Journey Mapping (CJM)** |
| Assumes segments are static, which they are rarely in digital markets | Use dynamic, data-driven **Marketing Mix Modelling** for real-time adjustments |
| Focuses heavily on the company's viewpoint of the market | Combine with **Jobs-To-Be-Done (JTBD)** to understand the customer's deeper motivations |
| Does not dictate the tactical execution of campaigns | Use the **4Ps / 7Ps Marketing Mix** to operationalize the STP strategy |
