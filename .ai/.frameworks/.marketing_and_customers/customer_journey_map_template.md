## Before You Start — Critical Instructions

> **The single most important rule of Journey Mapping:**
> _"A journey map is not about what your company processes are. It is about what the customer actually experiences. If you map your internal sales process and call it a customer journey, you have failed. You must map the journey from the outside in — starting with the customer's goal, not your business objective."_
> _(Source: Nielsen Norman Group — Journey Mapping Guide, 2025; IDEO — Human-Centered Design, 2025)_

**How to use this template:**

**Step 1** — Define the **Persona** and the specific **Scenario** you are mapping. A map trying to cover all customers for all scenarios is useless.
**Step 2** — Break the journey into logical **Phases** (usually 5–7 stages from awareness to advocacy).
**Step 3** — Map the **Actions, Touchpoints, and Thoughts** for each phase based on _real data_, not assumptions.
**Step 4** — Draw the **Emotion Curve** to identify the highs (moments of delight) and lows (pain points).
**Step 5** — Identify the **Moments of Truth (MoTs)** — the critical interactions that make or break the relationship.
**Step 6** — Create the **Action Plan** to fix the lows and elevate the MoTs.

> ⚠️ **Research warning:** The most common failure of journey mapping is "mapping by assumption" in a conference room without talking to real customers. A map without qualitative customer data (interviews, support logs, feedback) is just a corporate fantasy.
> _(Source: Harvard Business School — Customer Experience Mapping, 2025; Smaply — CJM Best Practices, 2025)_

> 🔁 **Review cycle:** Journey maps are living documents. Review and update them **every 6 months** or whenever you launch a major product update, change a core process, or enter a new market.
> _(Source: Qualtrics — Journey Management, 2025)_

## When to Use This Framework

| Situation                       | How This Template Helps                                                                                  |
| ------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| High customer churn rate        | Identifies exactly _where_ in the post-purchase phase customers are getting frustrated and leaving       |
| Low conversion rate on website  | Pinpoints the friction in the Consideration and Purchase phases                                          |
| Siloed organizations            | Forces Sales, Marketing, and Product teams to see the unified experience from the customer's perspective |
| Designing a new product/service | Allows you to design the _ideal_ future-state journey before building the product                        |
| Onboarding issues               | Helps map the critical first 30 days to ensure customers reach "Aha!" moments faster                     |

_(Source: Smaply — CJM Use Cases, 2025; IDEO — Design Thinking Applications, 2025)_

## Framework Limitations — Know What It Cannot Do

| Limitation                                                                        | What to Use Instead                                                                                |
| --------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Doesn't explain _why_ the customer has this underlying need in the first place    | Combine with **Jobs-To-Be-Done (JTBD)** to understand the core motivation                          |
| It is qualitative — it doesn't tell you the statistical frequency of the problems | Supplement with **Quantitative Analytics** (Google Analytics, Mixpanel, CSAT scores)               |
| A single map cannot represent all customers                                       | You must create separate maps for different **Personas** or distinct customer segments             |
| Does not fix internal operational processes                                       | Use a **Service Blueprint** to map the internal processes required to support the customer journey |

> _"The map is not the territory. The journey map is a simplified model of a complex human experience. Use it to build empathy and drive action, but don't mistake it for perfect reality."_
> _(Source: Nielsen Norman Group — Journey Mapping Limitations, 2025)_
