# E-Commerce Strategy Template
### Research-Backed Framework | Based on McKinsey, Gartner & Industry Data

> **How to use this template:**
> Each section includes what to write AND why it matters, backed by research.
> Fill every section before moving to execution. A strategy without all components is incomplete.
> Recommended review cycle: **quarterly** *(Source: Scandiweb / Wearedivisa, 2024)*

---

## 1. Executive Summary

**What to write:**
A 3–5 sentence snapshot of the entire strategy. Answer: Who are we? Who do we serve? What makes us different? What is our #1 measurable goal for the next 12 months?

> ⚠️ Write this section **last**, after completing all others.

**Why it matters:**
This is the alignment anchor for every team member and stakeholder. Without it, departments operate in silos and pursue conflicting priorities.
*(Source: Wearedivisa Ecommerce Strategy Roadmap, 2024)*

---

## 2. Vision & Mission

### Vision *(3–5 year horizon)*
**What to write:**
One ambitious but grounded sentence describing where you want the business to be in 3–5 years. Focus on market position, not revenue numbers.

> Example: *"To be the most trusted online destination for [product category] in [region] by [year]."*

### Mission *(Today's focus)*
**What to write:**
One or two sentences: what you do, for whom, and through what approach.

> Example: *"We help [audience] access [product/service] by offering [key differentiator]."*

**Why it matters:**
McKinsey research identifies brand vision as a prerequisite for D2C e-commerce success. Top leadership must define and commit to the strategic role before any execution begins.
*(Source: McKinsey — "The Six Must-Haves to Achieve Breakthrough Growth in E-Commerce D2C", 2021)*

---

## 3. Market & Customer Analysis

### 3.1 Target Market Definition
**What to write:**
Define your primary audience with specifics: age range, gender, location, income level, lifestyle, device usage, and shopping behavior. Then define a secondary audience if applicable.

> Research tip: Use Google Trends, Meta Audience Insights, or Semrush to validate assumptions with real data.

### 3.2 Customer Pain Points
**What to write:**
List 3–5 real frustrations your ideal customer has with current options. These become the foundation of your value proposition and messaging.

### 3.3 Customer Lifetime Value (LTV) Estimate
**What to write:**
Estimate what a single customer is worth over their entire relationship with your store.

> **Formula:** LTV = Average Order Value × Purchase Frequency × Retention Period (in months/years)

**Industry benchmark:**
A healthy LTV:CAC ratio for e-commerce businesses is **3:1** — meaning the lifetime value of a customer should be at least 3× what it costs to acquire them.
*(Source: Qubit Capital / First Page Sage, 2025–2026)*

### 4.2 Your Competitive Position

## 5. Value Proposition

**What to write:**
One statement that answers: *Why should a customer buy from YOU and not a competitor?*

> **Formula:** *"We help [target audience] [achieve/avoid something] by [your unique approach] — unlike [competitor], who [their limitation]."*

**What makes a strong value proposition:**
- Specific (not vague like "best quality" or "great service")
- Tied to a real customer pain point identified in Section 3
- Defensible — something you can actually deliver

**Why it matters:**
Gartner research shows companies prioritizing a customer-centric marketing strategy see a **20% increase in customer satisfaction and loyalty**. The value proposition is the foundation of that strategy.
*(Source: Gartner — "Ecommerce Strategy: CMOs' Outlook on the Future of Digital Commerce")*

## 6. Product & Pricing Strategy

### 6.1 Product Portfolio
**What to write:**
List your core product categories. Identify 1–3 "hero products" — high-margin or high-demand items that drive discovery and anchor the brand.

### 6.2 Pricing Strategy
**What to write:**
Choose your pricing model and justify it based on your positioning and target audience.

| Model | When to Use |
|---|---|
| **Premium pricing** | Strong brand, quality perception, niche market |
| **Competitive pricing** | Price-sensitive market, commodity products |
| **Value-based pricing** | Unique product, high perceived value |
| **Bundle/subscription** | Repeat purchase categories, retention-focused |

> ⚠️ Research caution: McKinsey warns that companies overemphasizing revenue over profitability damage long-term prospects. *"Unprofitable growth destroys value."* Build your pricing to protect margins, not just to win volume.
> *(Source: McKinsey — "A Path Toward Healthy, Sustainable Growth in E-Commerce", 2021)*

### 6.3 Inventory & Sourcing Model
**What to write:**
How do you source and manage stock? (Own inventory / Dropshipping / Print-on-demand / Wholesale / D2C from manufacturer)

## 7. Channel & Marketing Strategy

### 7.1 Sales Channels
**What to write:**
List every channel through which you will sell. Define the *role* of each channel — not just that it exists.

> **McKinsey insight:** 80% of winning e-commerce companies assign **distinct, clear roles** to each channel — different messaging, content, assortment, and pricing per channel.
> *(Source: McKinsey — "E-Commerce: At the Center of Profitable Growth", 2022)*

| Channel | Role (Reach / Revenue / Retention / Brand) | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Own website | | |
| Marketplace (Amazon, Noon, etc.) | | |
| Social commerce (TikTok Shop, Instagram) | | |
| Mobile app | | |

**Market context:**
By 2024, marketplaces accounted for **67% of global online sales**, up from 40% in 2014.
*(Source: Ecorn Agency — "Ecommerce Strategy Framework That Actually Drives Growth", 2025)*

### 7.2 Marketing & Acquisition Channels
**What to write:**
Rank your marketing channels by priority. For each channel, specify: target audience, goal (awareness / traffic / conversion), and monthly budget allocation.

| Channel | Goal | Monthly Budget | Target KPI |
|---|---|---|---|
| Instagram / TikTok Ads | | | |
| Google Search (SEO + PPC) | | | |
| Email Marketing | | | |
| Influencer Partnerships | | | |
| Content / Blog | | | |

> 💡 Research finding: The highest-ROI long-term channel for e-commerce is **SEO**, as it lowers Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) over time — unlike paid ads which scale in cost. Paid channels should be used for early traction, then diversified.
> *(Source: First Page Sage — "Average CAC for eCommerce Companies: 2026 Edition")*

### 7.3 Retention Strategy
**What to write:**
How will you keep customers coming back? Studies show retention is significantly cheaper than acquisition. Define your retention mechanics:

- Email / SMS flows (post-purchase, win-back, loyalty)
- Loyalty or points program
- Subscription offers
- Personalization approach

**Why it matters:**
Gartner research shows companies with a seamless digital commerce experience see a **25% increase in customer satisfaction and a 20% boost in repeat purchases**.
*(Source: Gartner — "Ecommerce Strategy: CMOs' Outlook on the Future of Digital Commerce")*

## 8. Technology & Platform Strategy

### 8.1 E-Commerce Platform
**What to write:**
Which platform will power your store, and why? Document your selection criteria (cost, scalability, integrations, ease of use).

> **Market context (2025):** Shopify holds **29%** global e-commerce platform market share, followed by Wix at 20% and WooCommerce at 17%. Over 28 million online stores exist globally.
> *(Source: Ecorn Agency, 2025)*

| Platform | Best For | Limitations |
|---|---|---|
| Shopify | Fast launch, scalability, apps ecosystem | Monthly fees, limited customization |
| WooCommerce | Flexibility, content-heavy stores | Requires technical maintenance |
| BigCommerce | Built-in features, B2B | Higher cost at scale |
| Custom-built | Unique requirements, enterprise | High cost, long development time |

### 8.2 Data & Analytics Stack
**What to write:**
List the tools you will use to collect, analyze, and act on data.

**Why this is non-negotiable:**
Gartner found that data-driven companies are **23% more likely to acquire new customers** and **19% more likely to retain existing ones**.
*(Source: Gartner — "Ecommerce Strategy: CMOs' Outlook on the Future of Digital Commerce")*

Minimum required tools: web analytics (GA4), heatmaps, email platform, ad attribution tool.

## 9. Operations & Fulfillment

### 9.1 Order Fulfillment Model
**What to write:**
How do orders get from you to the customer? (Self-fulfilled / 3PL warehouse / Dropship / Hybrid)

### 9.2 Shipping Strategy
**What to write:**
Which carriers? What are your delivery time targets? Free shipping threshold (if any)?

> Research note: McKinsey identifies logistics and fulfillment stability as *"critical to service quality and customer experience."* Last-mile delivery is often where brands lose or win.
> *(Source: McKinsey — "Becoming Indispensable: Moving Past E-Commerce to NeXT Commerce", 2022)*

### 9.3 Returns Policy
**What to write:**
Define your return/refund window, conditions, and process. A clear, fair policy reduces purchase hesitation.

### 9.4 Customer Support
**What to write:**
Channels available (email, chat, phone, WhatsApp), response time targets, and escalation process.

> McKinsey research on top digital performers shows they *"invest heavily in analyzing and mitigating any customer friction points and embracing a zero-defects mentality."*
> *(Source: McKinsey — "Think Fast: How to Accelerate E-Commerce Growth", 2020)*

## 11. Risk Management

**What to write:**
List 4–6 real risks. For each: how likely is it, what's the potential impact, and what's your mitigation plan?

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Low traffic at launch | High | High | Pre-launch content + influencer seeding |
| Rising ad costs / CAC inflation | High | Medium | Invest in SEO and email as owned channels |
| Supplier delays or stockouts | Medium | High | 2+ backup suppliers; safety stock policy |
| High cart abandonment | High | Medium | Abandoned cart email flows + checkout UX audit |
| Platform dependency (e.g. Amazon algorithm) | Medium | High | Build owned D2C channel in parallel |
| Data breach or fraud | Low | Very High | SSL, PCI compliance, fraud detection tools |

> **Context:** Customer acquisition costs have risen an average of **60% over the past five years**.
> *(Source: McKinsey — "Becoming Indispensable: Moving Past E-Commerce to NeXT Commerce", 2022)*

| KPI | Formula / Definition | Baseline | Target | Review |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Conversion Rate | Orders ÷ Sessions × 100 | | | Weekly |
| Average Order Value (AOV) | Revenue ÷ Orders | | | Weekly |
| Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) | Total Ad Spend ÷ New Customers | | | Monthly |
| LTV:CAC Ratio | LTV ÷ CAC | | ≥ 3:1 | Monthly |
| Cart Abandonment Rate | Abandoned Carts ÷ Initiated Carts | | | Weekly |
| Repeat Purchase Rate | Returning Buyers ÷ Total Buyers | | | Monthly |
| Return on Ad Spend (ROAS) | Revenue from Ads ÷ Ad Spend | | | Weekly |
