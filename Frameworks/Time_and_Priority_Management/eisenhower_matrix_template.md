## Before You Start — Critical Instructions

> **The single most important rule of the Eisenhower Matrix:**
> *"What is important is seldom urgent, and what is urgent is seldom important. Most people spend their lives reacting to 'Urgent' tasks (other people's priorities) while ignoring the 'Important' tasks (their own goals). Your primary objective is to spend as much time as possible in Quadrant 2 (Important, but Not Urgent)."*
> *(Source: Dwight D. Eisenhower; Stephen Covey — The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People)*

**How to use this template:**

**Step 1** — Brain dump: List absolutely everything on your to-do list right now.
**Step 2** — Filter by **Urgency**: Does this *have* to be done today/tomorrow, or else there will be immediate consequences?
**Step 3** — Filter by **Importance**: Does this actually move the needle on my long-term goals or core values?
**Step 4** — Place each task into one of the four quadrants below.
**Step 5** — Take action: Do, Schedule, Delegate, or Delete.

> ⚠️ **Research warning:** The "Urgency Trap" is real. We are biologically wired to respond to urgent things (like a ringing phone or pinging email) even if they are completely unimportant. You must consciously defend your time from Quadrant 3 (Urgent but Not Important).
> *(Source: TrackingTime — Time Management Psychology, 2025)*

> 🔁 **Review cycle:** Review and sort your matrix **daily** (first thing in the morning) or **weekly** (Sunday evening prep).

## SYNTHESIS: TIME ALLOCATION CHECK

*Look at where your tasks are clustered. What does your matrix reveal about how you manage time?*

| Your Current Reality | What it means | What you need to change |
|---|---|---|
| **Mostly Q1 (Do)** | You are living in crisis mode. High risk of burnout. | Move tasks to Q2 by planning ahead to prevent emergencies. |
| **Mostly Q2 (Schedule)** | **Ideal State.** You are proactive and strategic. | Keep protecting this time from Q3 interruptions. |
| **Mostly Q3 (Delegate)**| You suffer from the "Urgency Trap" and people-pleasing. | Practice saying "No." Empower your team. Turn off notifications. |
| **Mostly Q4 (Delete)** | You are avoiding actual work (procrastination). | Reconnect with your goals. Why are you avoiding Q1/Q2 tasks? |

## When to Use This Framework

| Situation | How This Template Helps |
|---|---|
| Overwhelm | Instantly reduces anxiety by proving you don't actually have to do everything today |
| Weekly Planning | Ensures that your long-term goals (Q2) actually make it onto your calendar before your week fills up with meetings (Q3) |
| Performance Reviews | Helps managers teach employees how to prioritize their own workloads |
| Startups/Founders | Prevents founders from getting bogged down in low-value admin tasks (Q3) instead of building the business (Q2) |

*(Source: Asana — Time Management, 2025; ActiveCollab — Task Prioritization, 2025)*

---

## Framework Limitations — Know What It Cannot Do

| Limitation | What to Use Instead |
|---|---|
| Does not tell you *how* to do the work, only when | Combine with the **Pomodoro Technique** or **Timeboxing** for actual execution |
| Can be difficult for junior employees who don't have the authority to Delegate or Delete | Junior employees should use this to negotiate workloads with managers: *"I can do X, but I will have to drop Y."* |
| Doesn't prioritize features in a product | Use the **MoSCoW Method** or **RICE Scoring** for product management |
