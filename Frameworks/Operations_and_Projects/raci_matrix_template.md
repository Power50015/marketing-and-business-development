## Before You Start — Critical Instructions

> **The single most important rule of the RACI Matrix:**
> *"There can be ONLY ONE 'Accountable' (A) person per task. If everyone is accountable, no one is accountable. The 'Accountable' person is the single point of failure who must sign off on the work. You can have many 'Responsible' (R) people doing the work, but only one 'A'."*
> *(Source: CIO.com — Project Accountability; Asana — RACI Best Practices, 2025)*

**How to use this template:**

**Step 1** — List the most critical tasks, milestones, or decisions in the left column. (Keep it to 8-15 major items to avoid "Alphabet Soup").
**Step 2** — List the roles or specific team members across the top row.
**Step 3** — Assign **R**, **A**, **C**, or **I** to the intersections.
**Step 4** — Perform the **Diagnostic Check** (at the bottom of this document) to ensure the matrix is balanced.
**Step 5** — Get alignment: Ensure everyone agrees with their assigned letters *before* the project starts.

> ⚠️ **Research warning (R vs A):** The most common mistake is confusing *Responsible* and *Accountable*. 
> - **R (Responsible):** The "Doer". The person actually typing the code, writing the document, or doing the design.
> - **A (Accountable):** The "Owner". The person who approves the work, takes the blame if it fails, and provides the final 'Yes/No'.
> *(Source: Invensis Learning — RACI Pitfalls, 2025)*

> 🔁 **Review cycle:** Review the RACI matrix at the start of every new project phase. As team compositions change, the matrix must be updated. It is a living document.

## THE RACI DEFINITIONS (Quick Reference)

| Letter | Role | Definition | Rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| **R** | **Responsible** | Does the actual work to complete the task. | Must have *at least one* per task. |
| **A** | **Accountable** | Owns the final result. Has yes/no/veto power. | Must have *exactly one* per task. |
| **C** | **Consulted** | Subject matter expert providing input *before* the work is done. | Keep this list as small as possible. |
| **I** | **Informed** | Needs to know the outcome *after* the work is done. | One-way communication. Do not ask for their approval. |

## SYNTHESIS: THE DIAGNOSTIC CHECK

*After filling out the matrix, review it vertically and horizontally. If you fail any of these checks, your project is at risk.*

### Horizontal Check (Task View)
1. **Are there any tasks with zero 'R's?** 
   *(If yes, the work won't get done. Assign a 'Doer').*
2. **Are there any tasks with zero 'A's?** 
   *(If yes, no one owns the outcome. Assign an 'Owner').*
3. **Are there any tasks with MORE THAN ONE 'A'?** 
   *(If yes, you will have decision-making gridlock. Choose ONE Accountable person. If you can't, break the task down into two smaller tasks).*
4. **Are there too many 'C's on a single task?** 
   *(If yes, you will suffer from "analysis paralysis." Limit consultants to essential experts).*

### Vertical Check (Role View)
1. **Does one person have too many 'R's?** 
   *(If yes, this person is a bottleneck and will burn out. Redistribute the work).*
2. **Does one person have zero 'R's or 'A's?** 
   *(If yes, does this person really need to be involved in the project at all?)*
3. **Is someone 'A' for every task?** 
   *(If yes, you are micromanaging. Delegate accountability downwards).*

## When to Use This Framework

| Situation | How This Template Helps |
|---|---|
| Projects with multiple departments | Prevents the classic "I thought Marketing was doing that?" "No, I thought Sales was doing that!" scenario. |
| Approval gridlock | Identifies exactly who has the final say (the 'A'), preventing endless committees from delaying decisions. |
| Onboarding new team members | Instantly shows a new hire exactly what they are doing, who they report to, and who they need to consult. |
| Stakeholder management | Keeps overly involved executives in the 'Informed' (I) category, preventing them from holding up production. |

*(Source: TeamGantt — RACI Applications, 2025; MindTools — RACI Diagnostics, 2025)*

---

## Framework Limitations — Know What It Cannot Do

| Limitation | What to Use Instead |
|---|---|
| Does not manage the project schedule or deadlines | Combine with a **Gantt Chart** or **Kanban Board** |
| Does not define the exact steps to complete the task | Use standard **SOPs (Standard Operating Procedures)** |
| In agile/scrum environments, strict RACIs can hinder self-organizing teams | In Agile, the whole squad is often 'Accountable' together. Use a modified RACI or standard Scrum roles (Product Owner, Scrum Master, Developer). |
