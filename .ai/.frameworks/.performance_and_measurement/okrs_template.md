## Before You Start — Critical Instructions

> **The single most important rule of OKRs:**
> _"An Objective is WHAT you want to achieve (Qualitative, Inspirational). A Key Result is HOW you will measure if you achieved it (Quantitative, Verifiable). If it does not have a number, it is not a Key Result."_
> _(Source: John Doerr — Measure What Matters; Andy Grove — High Output Management)_

**How to use this template:**

**Step 1** — Define 1 to 3 **Objectives** for the upcoming quarter (3 months). Do not create more than 3.
**Step 2** — For each Objective, define 3 to 5 **Key Results** (metrics that prove you achieved the objective).
**Step 3** — Assign an **Owner** to every Key Result.
**Step 4** — List the **Initiatives** (projects/tasks) you will execute to move the Key Results.
**Step 5** — Review progress weekly or bi-weekly using a **Confidence Score** (0-10).

> ⚠️ **Research warning (OKRs vs. KPIs):** A KPI (Key Performance Indicator) measures the ongoing _health_ of your business (e.g., Uptime = 99.9%). An OKR measures _change and growth_ (e.g., Objective: "Revolutionize our search speed"; Key Result: "Reduce latency from 200ms to 50ms"). Do not put "business-as-usual" tasks in your OKRs.
> _(Source: Monday.com — OKRs vs KPIs, 2025)_

> 🔁 **Review cycle:** Set OKRs **quarterly**. Check in on progress **weekly or bi-weekly**. At the end of the quarter, grade them and start fresh.

## STAGE 3: THE QUARTERLY GRADING (Retrospective)

_At the end of the quarter, grade your performance. Google's model suggests a score between 0.0 and 1.0._

- **0.7 to 1.0 (Green):** We delivered. _(Note: If you consistently hit 1.0, your goals are too easy. You should be setting "Stretch Goals")._
- **0.4 to 0.6 (Yellow):** We made progress, but fell short.
- **0.0 to 0.3 (Red):** We failed to make meaningful progress.

### Retrospective Questions

_Discuss these as a team before setting the next quarter's OKRs:_

1. **Did we accomplish all of our objectives? If not, why?**
   >
2. **Were our Key Results the right things to measure? Did they actually reflect success?**
   >
3. **If we were to rewrite these OKRs today, what would we change?**
   >
4. **What should we carry over into the next quarter? What should we drop?**
   >

## The OKR "Superpowers" Checklist

_Review your draft OKRs against John Doerr's four superpowers:_

☐ **1. Focus:** Do we have 3 or fewer Objectives? Are we saying "no" to good ideas to focus on great ones?
☐ **2. Alignment:** Does every team member know how their specific OKRs connect to the top-level company strategy?
☐ **3. Tracking:** Do we have a system (e.g., this document, Notion, Smartsheet) to track weekly progress without heavy administration?
☐ **4. Stretching:** Are these goals slightly uncomfortable? Will a 70% completion rate still represent an amazing achievement?

## When to Use This Framework

| Situation                           | How This Template Helps                                                                                                             |
| ----------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Scaling a fast-growing startup      | Keeps remote or expanding teams strictly aligned on the 3 things that actually matter this quarter                                  |
| Shifting from "Output" to "Outcome" | Stops teams from celebrating "shipping a feature" (Output) and forces them to measure "did the feature increase revenue?" (Outcome) |
| Agile environments                  | Allows companies to pivot strategy every 90 days, unlike rigid annual planning models                                               |

_(Source: Asana — OKR Implementations, 2025; IBM — Strategic Alignment, 2025)_

---

## Framework Limitations — Know What It Cannot Do

| Limitation                                                        | What to Use Instead                                                                                                                            |
| ----------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Not for measuring daily operational health or "business as usual" | Use **KPIs (Key Performance Indicators)** or a **Balanced Scorecard**                                                                          |
| Should NOT be tied directly to employee compensation or bonuses   | Use traditional **Performance Appraisals / MBOs**. If you tie OKRs to bonuses, employees will "sandbag" (set easy goals to guarantee payouts). |
| Does not replace a company vision or long-term strategy           | Use the **Vision/Mission Framework** or **Value Disciplines Model** for 3-5 year planning                                                      |
