## Before You Start — Critical Instructions

> **The single most important rule of the Balanced Scorecard:**
> *"What you measure is what you get. If you only measure financial outcomes, you are driving by looking in the rearview mirror. The BSC forces you to measure the 'Leading Indicators' (Learning, Process, Customer) that *cause* the 'Lagging Indicators' (Financial success) to happen."*
> *(Source: Kaplan & Norton — The Balanced Scorecard; BSC Institute — Strategic Execution, 2025)*

**How to use this template:**

**Step 1** — Define your overarching **Strategic Theme or Vision**.
**Step 2** — Build the **Strategy Map** from the bottom up (Learning & Growth ➔ Internal Process ➔ Customer ➔ Financial).
**Step 3** — For each perspective, define 2-3 **Strategic Objectives**.
**Step 4** — Assign **KPIs (Key Performance Indicators)** to measure success, ensuring a mix of Leading and Lagging indicators.
**Step 5** — Define the specific **Initiatives** (projects/actions) that will move the KPIs.

> ⚠️ **Research warning:** Do not list more than 15 KPIs across the entire scorecard. A scorecard with 50 metrics is not a strategy; it is a chaotic operational dashboard. Focus only on the metrics that directly link to your strategic objectives.
> *(Source: Intrafocus — BSC Best Practices, 2025)*

> 🔁 **Review cycle:** Review KPI progress **monthly**. Review and update the strategic objectives and initiatives **annually** or during major market shifts.

## SYNTHESIS: THE CAUSALITY CHECK

*A good BSC tells a logical story of cause and effect. Read your scorecard from the bottom up to ensure the logic holds.*

**The "If / Then" Test:**
> **IF** we execute `[Initiative from Learning & Growth]`
> **THEN** we will achieve `[KPI Target in Learning & Growth]`
> **WHICH WILL CAUSE** our `[KPI in Internal Process]` to improve
> **WHICH WILL CAUSE** our `[KPI in Customer]` to increase
> **WHICH ULTIMATELY RESULTS IN** hitting our `[Financial Target]`.

**Diagnostic Question:** *Does the "If / Then" story logically hold up, or are our metrics disconnected from one another?*

## When to Use This Framework

| Situation | How This Template Helps |
|---|---|
| Transitioning from a startup to a mature enterprise | Forces leadership to look beyond cash flow and start building long-term organizational capacity |
| Strategy execution failure | Solves the problem of having a great vision but no way to measure if daily operations are actually supporting it |
| Departmental silos | Forces IT, HR, Operations, and Finance to see how their specific metrics rely on each other to succeed |
| Annual Strategic Planning | Translates vague annual goals ("Be the best") into concrete, measurable projects ("Achieve 60 NPS by Q3") |

*(Source: Monday.com — BSC Implementations, 2025; Intrafocus — Strategic Execution, 2025)*

---

## Framework Limitations — Know What It Cannot Do

| Limitation | What to Use Instead |
|---|---|
| Slow to adapt to highly volatile, fast-moving tech markets | Use **OKRs (Objectives and Key Results)** for rapid, quarterly goal-setting |
| Does not tell you *what* your strategy should be, only how to measure it | Use **Porter's Five Forces**, **SWOT**, or the **Value Disciplines Model** to formulate the strategy first |
| Can become a bureaucratic reporting nightmare if overcomplicated | Stick to the "Rule of 15" (Max 15 KPIs total) to keep it actionable |
