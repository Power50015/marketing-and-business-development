## Before You Start — Critical Instructions

> **The single most important rule of the Value Disciplines Model:**
> *"You cannot be everything to everyone. To achieve market leadership, you must choose ONE of the three value disciplines to excel in, while maintaining 'table-stakes' (industry average) performance in the other two. Companies that try to be the cheapest, the most innovative, AND the most customized end up stuck in the middle, achieving none."*
> *(Source: Treacy & Wiersema — The Discipline of Market Leaders, 1995; Umbrex — Strategic Frameworks, 2025)*

**How to use this template:**

**Step 1** — Complete the **Diagnostic Assessment** to understand your current organizational bias.
**Step 2** — Analyze your competitors to identify the **Market Baseline (Thresholds)**.
**Step 3** — **The Decision:** Explicitly choose your Primary Discipline.
**Step 4** — Complete the **Alignment Audit** to ensure your operations, culture, and metrics support your choice.

> ⚠️ **Research warning:** The biggest mistake companies make with this model is failing to maintain the "thresholds." If you choose *Product Leadership* (like Apple), you don't have to be the cheapest, but your price still must be acceptable to your target market. If you choose *Operational Excellence* (like Walmart), you don't need cutting-edge innovation, but your products can't be completely obsolete.
> *(Source: Soren Kaplan — Strategic Focus, 2025)*

> 🔁 **Review cycle:** Changing a value discipline requires completely rebuilding your company's operating model and culture. This is a long-term strategic decision. Review alignment **annually**, but do not flip disciplines from year to year.

## THE THREE DISCIPLINES — Quick Reference

| Discipline | The Goal | The Promise | Example Companies |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Operational Excellence** | Efficiency, scale, low cost, reliability | *"We offer the lowest total cost and best convenience."* | Amazon, Walmart, McDonald's, IKEA |
| **Product Leadership** | Innovation, performance, design | *"We offer the best, most innovative product."* | Apple, Tesla, Nike, Dyson |
| **Customer Intimacy** | Deep relationships, customization, high-touch | *"We provide the best solution tailored specifically to you."* | Salesforce, Ritz-Carlton, McKinsey |

## When to Use This Framework

| Situation | How This Template Helps |
|---|---|
| Formulating corporate strategy | Provides a clear lens to decide what kind of company you want to be over the next 5-10 years |
| Resolving internal conflict | Stops arguments between departments (e.g., Sales wanting customization vs. Ops wanting standardization) by declaring a primary discipline |
| M&A (Mergers & Acquisitions) | Evaluates whether a target company shares your fundamental operating model and culture |
| Brand messaging | Clarifies your core promise to the market, ensuring marketing doesn't promise intimacy if operations are built for low-cost volume |

*(Source: Forefront Consulting — Value Disciplines in Action, 2025)*

## Framework Limitations — Know What It Cannot Do

| Limitation | What to Use Instead |
|---|---|
| Does not map the specific customer segment or persona | Combine with the **STP Framework** and **Customer Journey Map** |
| Does not explain how to execute the specific innovation or operational changes | Combine with **Agile/Scrum** (for Product Leadership) or **Lean/Six Sigma** (for Operational Excellence) |
| Too broad for early-stage startups seeking initial product-market fit | Start with the **Lean Canvas** and adopt a Value Discipline as you scale |
