## Before You Start — Critical Instructions

> **The single most important rule of the Value Proposition Canvas:**
> _"Start with the Customer Profile (the circle on the right), NOT your Value Map (the square on the left). If you start with your product features, you will inevitably 'reverse-engineer' fake customer needs to justify the product you already built. The goal is to build products that solve real customer jobs — not to find customers for your product."_
> _(Source: Strategyzer — Value Proposition Design, 2025; Luth Research — VPC Applications, 2025)_

**How to use this template:**

**Step 1** — Identify the specific **Customer Segment** you are analyzing. (Do not mix segments; create a separate canvas for each).
**Step 2** — Complete the **Customer Profile (Right Side)**: Identify Jobs, Pains, and Gains. Then _rank them_ by importance.
**Step 3** — Complete the **Value Map (Left Side)**: Identify your Products/Services, Pain Relievers, and Gain Creators.
**Step 4** — Evaluate the **"Fit"**: Do your Pain Relievers and Gain Creators directly address the highest-ranked Pains and Gains of the customer?
**Step 5** — Write the final **Value Proposition Statement**.

> ⚠️ **Research warning:** A common mistake is treating "Gains" as simply the opposite of "Pains" (e.g., Pain: "It's too slow", Gain: "It's fast"). Pains are the obstacles blocking the job. Gains are the _positive outcomes or unexpected delights_ the customer hopes to achieve when the job is done.
> _(Source: B2B International — VPC Common Mistakes, 2025)_

> 🔁 **Review cycle:** The VPC is a hypothesis tool. You must review it **monthly** during product discovery to ensure your assumptions about customer Pains and Gains are validated by actual user interviews.

## When to Use This Framework

| Situation                     | How This Template Helps                                                                                |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Developing a new product      | Ensures you are building features that actually solve customer problems (achieving Product-Market Fit) |
| Improving an existing product | Identifies features that don't relieve pain or create gains, allowing you to deprecate them            |
| Creating marketing copy       | Provides the exact words and emotional triggers you need for your website and ad copy                  |
| Re-aligning teams             | Stops arguments between Product and Marketing by anchoring decisions to real customer evidence         |

_(Source: Client Factory — VPC Use Cases, 2025; ThinkInsights — Achieving Fit, 2025)_

## Framework Limitations — Know What It Cannot Do

| Limitation                                                        | What to Use Instead                                                                      |
| ----------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Does not address the financial viability (how you make money)     | Combine with the **Business Model Canvas (BMC)** or **Lean Canvas**                      |
| Does not map the specific channels or customer journey            | Combine with **Customer Journey Mapping (CJM)**                                          |
| Does not explain the specific forces driving a customer to switch | Use the **Jobs-To-Be-Done (JTBD) Forces Diagram**                                        |
| Assumes you have accurate customer data                           | If you don't, you must conduct **Customer Discovery Interviews** before filling this out |

> _"The Value Proposition Canvas is a plug-in to the Business Model Canvas. The VPC helps you design great value propositions that match customer needs, while the BMC helps you create a profitable and scalable business model around those value propositions."_
> _(Source: Alexander Osterwalder — Strategyzer, 2025)_
