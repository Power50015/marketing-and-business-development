## Before You Start — Critical Instructions

> **The single most important rule of SMART Goals:**
> *"A goal without a timeline is just a dream, and a goal without a metric is just a wish. The purpose of the SMART framework is to force you to define exactly what 'done' looks like. If two people look at your goal at the end of the year and disagree on whether you achieved it, your goal was not SMART."*
> *(Source: Forbes — SMART Goal Setting, 2025; George Doran — Management Review, 1981)*

**How to use this template:**

**Step 1** — Write down your initial, vague goal (e.g., "I want to grow the business").
**Step 2** — Run the goal through the **S.M.A.R.T.** criteria filters below.
**Step 3** — Synthesize the answers into one clear, actionable statement.
**Step 4** — Establish your **E.R.** (Evaluate & Readjust) routine to ensure the goal doesn't die in a drawer.

> ⚠️ **Research warning:** In 2025, the standard SMART framework evolved into **SMARTER**. The world changes too fast to set a 12-month goal and blindly stick to it if the market shifts. You must build in regular **E**valuation and be willing to **R**eadjust your methods (without giving up on the ultimate vision).
> *(Source: Positive Psychology — SMARTER Goals, 2025)*

> 🔁 **Review cycle:** You must actively *Evaluate* (E) your progress **monthly** or **quarterly** to decide if you need to *Readjust* (R).

## When to Use This Framework

| Situation | How This Template Helps |
|---|---|
| Employee Performance Reviews | Replaces vague feedback ("You need to communicate better") with objective targets ("You will lead one team meeting per month for the next quarter") |
| Personal Development | Transforms wishes ("I want to learn coding") into actionable plans ("I will complete the Python course on Codecademy by Nov 1st by studying 4 hours a week") |
| Project Kickoffs | Ensures the entire team agrees on the exact definition of "done" before any money is spent or code is written |

*(Source: ClickUp — Goal Setting, 2025; AIHR — Strategic Planning, 2025)*

---

## Framework Limitations — Know What It Cannot Do

| Limitation | What to Use Instead |
|---|---|
| Does not align multiple teams across a company | Use **OKRs (Objectives and Key Results)** to cascade and align goals company-wide |
| Can discourage "Moonshot" thinking | Because SMART goals must be "Achievable," they can sometimes cause teams to set safe, mediocre targets. Use **BHAGs (Big Hairy Audacious Goals)** for 10-year vision planning. |
| Does not track daily habits | Use a **Habit Tracker** or **Timeboxing** for daily behavioral changes |
